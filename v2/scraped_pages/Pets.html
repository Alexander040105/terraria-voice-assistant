Not to be confused with Town pets, the animal NPCs obtainable via the Zoologist.

Pets are creatures that follow the player around. They are completely invincible and have no set duration. When summoned, they will give the player a buff with the same name as the pet. A pet will follow the player until they die, summon a different pet, leave the world or cancel the associated buff.

Most pets are purely cosmetic, but some of the  Old-gen console,  Windows Phone, and 3DS-exclusive pets can do minimal damage to enemies. A few pets emit small amounts of light, but on  PC version,  Console version,  Mobile version,  Old Chinese version,  tModLoader version, and  tModLoader 1.3-Legacy version, there are special Light Pets available with their own dedicated slot.

Summoned minions operate similarly, but are primarily intended to aid combat rather than being cosmetic. Pets do not count against a player's current minion limit.

In total, there are 59 pets. There are 48 on the  PC version,  Console version,  Mobile version, and  tModLoader version, 26 on the  tModLoader 1.3-Legacy version, 27 on the  Old-gen console version, and 31 on the Nintendo 3DS version.

There are also 10 light pets on the  PC version,  Console version,  Mobile version, and  tModLoader version, 3 of which are master-exclusive, 7 on the  tModLoader 1.3-Legacy version, and 3 on the  Old-gen console version,  Windows Phone version, and Nintendo 3DS version.

Quick Answers

What are the benefits of having a pet in Terraria?
In Terraria, pets are creatures that follow the player around, providing a fun and engaging visual element to your gameplay. They are completely invincible and have no set duration, meaning they'll stick with you through thick and thin. When summoned, they give the player a buff with the same name as the pet. While most pets are purely cosmetic, some versions of the game feature pets with more complex behaviors. However, it's important to note that pets do not provide any gameplay advantages, they're just there for the visual appeal and the joy of having a cute companion on your adventures!
Provided by: Fandom
Which pets in Terraria can do minimal damage to enemies?
In Terraria, most pets are purely cosmetic and do not deal any damage to enemies. They are invincible creatures that follow the player around for an indefinite duration, providing a buff with the same name as the pet. However, there are some exceptions in certain versions of the game, but these are not the norm.
Provided by: Fandom
What are some of the best pets in Terraria and why?
Pets in Terraria are more than just cute followers, they can also provide various benefits to the player. Some of the best pets include the Alien Skater, Baby Ogre, Destroyer-Lite, Eater of Worms, and Everscream Sapling. These pets are considered the best due to their unique appearances and the fun factor they bring to the game. However, it's important to note that most pets in Terraria are purely cosmetic and do not provide any gameplay advantages. For those looking for utility, light pets like the Jack 'O Lantern, Toy Golem, and Fairy Princess can provide illumination in dark areas, making them a practical choice for spelunking and night exploration.
Provided by: Fandom
How does a pet in Terraria provide light?
In Terraria, light pets are a special type of pet that emit light, aiding players in dark areas or while caving. These pets are summoned using specific items and once summoned, they follow the player around indefinitely, even in liquids, without costing any mana. They are infinitely reusable and have their own dedicated inventory slot in the player's equipment section. Some light pets can even be controlled by the player. However, while it's possible to change the color of the light pet itself using dyes, the color of the light they produce will always remain the same.
Provided by: Fandom
What happens when you summon a different pet in Terraria?
In Terraria, when you summon a different pet, the current pet following you will be replaced by the new one. Pets will follow the player around until they die, summon a different pet, leave the world, or cancel the associated buff. So, if you decide to summon a different pet, your current pet will simply disappear and the new pet will start following you around.
Provided by: Fandom

Summoning and Dismissing[]

Pets are manually summoned by placing their item on the hotbar and using them or by equipping them. On  PC,  Console,  Old-gen console,  Mobile,  Old Chinese,  tModLoader, and  tModLoader Legacy, pets can be dismissed by pressing the ⚷ Interact button on their corresponding buff icon or by turning off the visibility.

Equipping Pets[]

     
PC/Console/Mobile/Old Chinese/tModLoader/tModLoader 1.3-Legacy-Only Content: This section's information applies only to the PC, Console, Mobile, Old Chinese, tModLoader, and tModLoader Legacy versions of Terraria.

On the  PC version,  Console version,  Mobile version,  tModLoader version, and  tModLoader 1.3-Legacy version, the inventory offers two dedicated slots, offering more complex behavior: One slot is for "regular" pets, the other is for light pets. Light pets behave similarly to ordinary pets, but provide more consistent lighting around the player. At any time, the player can have at most one pet of each type active.

  • Each pet slot has a matching dye slot, which can be used to recolor the pet. Some of the "advanced" dyes can make certain pets look very strange, or even render them invisible. If they normally shed light, the light will be unaffected by any dye.
  • Each pet slot also has an "eye" icon, which can be clicked to "hide" the pet, and again to "show" it.
    • Unlike the similar icons to hide accessories, the eye icon will actually dismiss and resummon the pet, not just hide and reveal it.
    • If a summoning item is placed in a slot set to "hidden", the pet will not be summoned until the slot is toggled back to "visible".
  • Removing a pet's item from its slot will not dismiss the pet, but placing a different pet item in the slot will replace the pet.
  • Either type of pet can also be summoned by using its item from the hotbar, but only if the slot it could go into is empty or hidden.
  • A pet in a pet slot is automatically summoned whenever the player respawns, as long as the "eye" icon is set to visible.

Light Pets[]

Light Pets are summoned secondary characters that float alongside players to provide a traveling light source. They incur no mana nor any other cost, and remain indefinitely until the player cancels the buff, summons a replacement light pet or dies. Only one light pet can be present at once. Light pets have their own dedicated inventory slot in the player's equipment section in the  PC version,  Console version,  Mobile version,  Old Chinese version,  tModLoader version, and  tModLoader 1.3-Legacy version along with a slot for dyes.

Notes[]

  • All pets can activate Teal Pressure Pads.
  • Although it is possible to change the color of the light pet itself using dyes, the color of the light they produce will stay the same.

Achievement[]

Pet Hoarder • “You seem to like pets.”
Obtain all exclusive pets.

Tips[]

  • There are four pets that do something other than being cosmetic. The Cursed Sapling, Baby Truffle and Volt Bunny provide light for the player, which is rather useful when caving or in dark areas. Meanwhile, the Eye Bone lets the player access their piggy bank without having to place one down.

Trivia[]

  • The pet summoned is actually a long-lasting projectile that follows the player around.
  • The Pet Drone, summoned with the Mysterious Package, was introduced only on the Amazon version, but was unobtainable and later removed.
  • Upon updating to 1.3, all exclusive pets were replaced with a Gold Bunny each.
